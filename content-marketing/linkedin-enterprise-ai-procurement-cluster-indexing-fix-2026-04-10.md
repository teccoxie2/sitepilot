# LinkedIn Draft — Enterprise AI Procurement Content Needs an Indexable Buying Path

Most enterprise AI content dies because it looks informative but does nothing for a real buying team.

If a page cannot answer three questions fast — what this asset is, where it sits in the buying process, and what page comes next — it weakens both SEO and procurement usability.

That is why we keep pushing SitePilot’s enterprise AI vendor procurement cluster toward a stricter structure:

- 40–60 word factual opening paragraphs
- BOFU intent instead of vague “AI trends” commentary
- clear next-step links from comparison → RFP → due diligence → scorecard → pricing → pilot
- page-level metadata that actually renders, not decorative files that Next.js ignores
- sitemap inclusion for decision-stage pages, not just top-level guides

This week’s cleanup surfaced a common mistake: a perfectly good procurement asset can still underperform if it is missing from the sitemap or if page metadata is wired incorrectly.

Content quality matters. But crawlability and sequence logic matter too.

If your enterprise AI content library is generating impressions without producing qualified buying movement, the problem is usually structural — not just editorial.

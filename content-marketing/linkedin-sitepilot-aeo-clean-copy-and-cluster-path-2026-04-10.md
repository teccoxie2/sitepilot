# LinkedIn Draft — Clean Copy Wins More Than Loud Copy (2026-04-10)

Most SEO teams still make the same dumb mistake.

They publish BOFU pages with fake proof, inflated counts, and adjectives doing all the work.

That hurts both trust and extractability.

We just tightened another pass on SitePilot’s procurement and proxy clusters with a simpler rule:

- factual opening first
- evidence-led wording second
- cluster path third
- hype last, preferably never

A few examples of what got cleaned up:

- removed unsupported numeric proof language from the enterprise AI vendor comparison guide
- removed leftover hype phrasing from the proxy hardware guide
- kept the internal decision path intact so readers can move from methodology to comparison to action pages

The practical AEO lesson is boring, which is why it works:

1. Open with a 40-60 word factual definition
2. Keep BOFU intent obvious
3. Replace invented proof with defensible decision language
4. Build topic closure with real internal links

Answer engines are much more likely to trust content that sounds like an operator wrote it instead of a landing-page intern on caffeine.

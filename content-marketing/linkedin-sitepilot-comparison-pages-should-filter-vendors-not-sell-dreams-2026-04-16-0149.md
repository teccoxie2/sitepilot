Enterprise AI comparison pages are supposed to filter vendors.
They are not supposed to close the deal.

If your comparison content stops at features, star ratings, and polished demos, you are not helping procurement.
You are just giving vendor theater a nicer layout.

A useful comparison page should do four things:
- narrow the shortlist
- expose architecture and governance differences
- surface pricing and lock-in risk early
- push the buyer into RFP, due diligence, scorecard, and pilot validation

That is the difference between TOFU noise and BOFU decision support.

We tightened SitePilot’s comparison path again this week because answer engines extract what the production page actually says, not what the content brief intended.
If the page does not open with clear facts and connect into the next decision step, it leaks buying intent.

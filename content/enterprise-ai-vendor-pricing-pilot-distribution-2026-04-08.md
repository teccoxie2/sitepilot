# Enterprise AI Vendor Pricing + Pilot Distribution Pack

## Core angle
Enterprise AI vendor approval breaks when pricing is reviewed in one meeting and pilot evidence is reviewed in another. The fix is to connect commercial assumptions, pilot acceptance criteria, and shortlist scoring into one evidence chain.

## LinkedIn post
Enterprise AI deals get expensive when teams treat pricing and pilot validation as separate conversations.

One meeting reviews token pricing, seat models, minimum commits, and support tiers.
Another meeting reviews the pilot and says the tool “felt promising.”

That gap is where overage risk, lock-in, and hand-wavy vendor claims survive.

A cleaner approach:
1. pressure-test pricing assumptions before the pilot
2. convert unresolved claims into measurable pilot acceptance criteria
3. feed real pilot results back into the shortlist scorecard

## Reddit / community angle
If a team asks how to avoid choosing an AI vendor on demo energy alone, the practical answer is:
- force every pricing claim into a written assumption
- turn unresolved claims into pilot tests
- measure actual latency, usage, support behavior, and admin burden
- score the result against pre-agreed commercial and governance criteria
- reject finalists that still rely on vague future promises

## Core angle
Enterprise AI vendor selection should end with a weighted shortlist scorecard tied to documented RFP and due diligence evidence, not gut feel after polished demos.

## LinkedIn post
Most enterprise AI vendor selections go wrong in the last mile.

Teams do the RFP.
Teams do security review.
Teams do legal review.

Then somebody walks into the final meeting and picks the vendor with the best demo energy.

That is how expensive ambiguity survives all the “process.”

A better pattern is simple:
1. filter with an RFP
2. validate controls with due diligence
3. rank finalists with a weighted scorecard

I put together a practical shortlist scorecard for 2026 that scores AI vendors across strategic fit, architecture, data governance, security, commercial model, implementation speed, compliance, and lock-in risk.

## Reddit / community angle
If a team asks how to choose between the last 2-4 AI vendors on the table, the useful answer is not “book one more demo.” It is:
- define weighted criteria before the meeting
- score only against written evidence
- separate pass/fail controls from weighted differentiators
- reject finalists that stay vague on training usage, audit logging, deletion, portability, or support commitments
